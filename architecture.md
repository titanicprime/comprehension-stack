# Comprehension Stack Architecture

The Comprehension Stack is a layered civilizational architecture designed to strengthen human and institutional sensemaking.

It consists of five substrate layers:

---

## 1. Human Cognition Layer  
**Repository:** comprehension-scope  
**Purpose:** Provide individuals with a minimal, universal protocol for parsing claims, incentives, and manipulation.  
**Output:** Agency-preserving comprehension.

---

## 2. Shared Knowledge Layer  
**Repository:** clarity-index  
**Purpose:** Create a structured, verifiable reference of terms, definitions, and claims.  
**Output:** A stable epistemic backbone.

---

## 3. Trust & Attestation Layer  
**Repository:** bitrep-core  
**Purpose:** Represent attestations, provenance, and reliability without ideology or scoring.  
**Output:** A minimal trust substrate.

---

## 4. Governance Layer  
**Repository:** truth-freedom-agency-protocol  
**Purpose:** Define minimal rules of engagement for AI agents and human–AI interaction.  
**Output:** Predictable, interpretable agent behavior.

---

## 5. Safety Layer  
**Repository:** moltbot-safe  
**Purpose:** Provide hardened, practical implementations of safe agent behavior.  
**Output:** Operational safety primitives.

---

# Invariants

Across all layers, the following invariants hold:

- **Neutrality:** No embedded ideology or worldview  
- **Agency Preservation:** Never reduce the user’s feasible action space  
- **Interpretive Minimalism:** Provide structure, not conclusions  
- **Modularity:** Each layer is independent and replaceable  
- **Universality:** Works across cultures, politics, and institutions  
- **Adversarial Robustness:** Must withstand manipulation and noise  

These invariants define the identity of the Comprehension Stack.
